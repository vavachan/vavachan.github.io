Brief summaries of my research activities

Study of jamming transition under shear

The jamming transition is associated with what one normally associates with the word jamming - "traffic jams", or clogging. In a jammed system no collective motion of particles is possible, this in turn implies that the system will resist all deformation. Jamming is typically studied in numerical simulations by subjecting repulsive sphere packings to compression. Jamming can also occur when packings are subjected to shear deformation. My PhD thesis deals with this phenomena of shear jamming and the role of friction in it.

Rigidity transition and jamming

A graph becomes rigid when the no vertex of the graph can be moved without changing the length of the edges in the graph. For \(D=2\), a graph with \(N\) vertices and \( N_c \) edges, one needs (at least) \(N_c=2 N -2 \) edges to constrain all degrees of freedom (2 translational degrees of freedom cannot be constrained in periodic system). However this is a necessary but not a sufficient condition for rigidity as the edges need to be used "judiciously" - a subgraph should not contain more edges than required to ensure rigidity. Previous work, on bond diluted graphs show that the system acquires rigidity continously, while the contact network of particles undergoing jamming shows an abrupt jump in the size of the rigid cluster at the isostatic point \( N_c = 2N -2\). Our results show that when the system is composed of repulsive disks, rigidity does come about abruptly and these systems naturally makes a judicious use of bonds.

Force Tile Network

In a jammed configurations each particle is in force-balance - the net force acting on the particle is zero. If the was not zero then that particle is moving, which means that the system is not jammed. When the sum of forces acting on a particle is zero, the forces can be arranged in a way such that they form a closed loop. Therefore each particle has a corresponding force-tile. Now each force is shared between two particles, therefore the force-tiles of two particles has to share an edge. One can build the force-tile network for the whole jammed system this way. I had a lot of fun writing this code in python.
